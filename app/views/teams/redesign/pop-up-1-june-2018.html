NHS.UK Redesign Popup, Leeds library - 1 June 2018

Date conducted: 1 June 2018
Testing carried out by: Emma Holmes

Questions asked

  1. Do users notice the warning call out in relation to other content on the page?
  2. Are they seeing it with its intended prominence related to other content on page?
  3. Do they understand what it means?

Method

Popup testing in the entrance of Leeds library.

We tested the first iteration of a 'warning callout' on a medicines page prototype on an iPhone 6

We set the user a task to find a specific information on the page then asked specific questions regarding the 'warning callout'

Discussion guide (word document)

Version 1 prototype

Findings

We spoke to 33 users in total.

  • Users didn’t understand it as a warning
  • Some users didn’t see it as part of the page

Version 1 of the warning callout didn't answer the question.

Read the full report (PDF)

Next steps

Iterate the design based on the following hypthesis:

If we make the yellow brighter and increase the font weight
Then users will take more notice and read it
Because it looks more like a warning

Then test again
